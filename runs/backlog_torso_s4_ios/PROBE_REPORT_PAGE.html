Premise

The researcher has framed a study examining how a mobile recreation of an MPC-style hardware sampler interface creates tension between touchscreen interaction conventions (scroll, long-press, modal sheets) and the hardware's velocity-sensitive, eyes-free, two-handed tactile loop. [RESEARCHER_INPUT]

The sharpened question this guidebook addresses is: when mobile-native novice beatmakers who have never touched an MPC encounter an MPC-faithful mobile app, which hardware-derived interaction idioms do they spontaneously reinvent as mobile conventions, and which do they reject as foreign? [AGENT_INFERENCE]

This study is formative and generative. It is designed to surface design hypotheses and case descriptions about hardware-to-mobile idiom transfer. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

This study cannot support a generalizable taxonomy of "transfer-tolerant" versus "transfer-resistant" idioms, nor population-level convergence claims about mobile-native beatmakers. [DO_NOT_CLAIM]

This study cannot support claims about MPC-experienced producers, disabled mobile producers using assistive technology, or long-term adoption behavior. [DO_NOT_CLAIM]

Background

Sanders and Stappers position generative toolkits and prototypes within a "designing with people" participatory mindset, distinct from the "designing for people" mindset, and characterize such instruments as making approaches within codesign rather than as data-extraction instruments. [SOURCE_CARD:sanders_stappers_2014]

The same authors frame probes as instruments for inspiration and dialogue rather than for extracting generalizable user data — a framing that should constrain how results from a six-participant workshop are claimed. [SOURCE_CARD:sanders_stappers_2014]

Gaver, Dunne, and Pacenti introduced cultural probes as packages of artefacts designed to elicit inspirational fragments from participants, embracing uncertainty and partial returns rather than seeking comprehensive coverage of user behavior. [SOURCE_CARD:gaver_dunne_pacenti_1999]

Illich's distinction between convivial and industrial tools — whether a tool expands or contracts the range of autonomous action available to its users — offers a frame for asking which hardware-derived idioms afford or foreclose novice mobile agency when ported to glass. [SOURCE_CARD:illich_1973]

Method

Probe does not run a study. It writes one, rehearses it, and audits the rehearsal. This section restates the prototype specification — the artifact a different researcher could build from without asking clarifying questions — and the study protocol.

The prototype is a 120-minute co-design workshop held in a quiet usability lab with one participant, one facilitator, one wizard, and one silent observer. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

The participant sits at a central table with an iPad Pro running a Figma prototype in kiosk mode; the wizard operates a mirrored Figma file from a secondary workstation 1.5 m behind the participant and advances screen states in response to participant gestures relayed through a pre-agreed hand-signal vocabulary. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

The Figma prototype contains a base state (16-pad grid, step sequencer, bank buttons A–D, shift button), 16 individual pad-highlight frames, a shift-chord overlay layer, four bank color variants, and five conflict-card variant screens corresponding to common mobile patterns (long-press overlay, mode-switch toggle, swipe-to-reveal, floating action button, radial context menu). [AGENT_INFERENCE]

The session proceeds through five phases: (1) welcome and consent, (2) silent orientation videos of MPC hardware and the mobile app, (3) fifteen minutes of free exploration of the Figma prototype, (4) conflict-card co-design in which the participant sketches and enacts alternatives to five hardware-derived idioms, and (5) a structured debrief. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

The five conflict cards target: shift-chord two-handed secondary function access, bank switching between four sample banks, pad long-hold for sample assignment, step-lane horizontal scroll for sequence extension, and simultaneous multi-pad press for chord triggering. Each card describes the hardware behavior and the mobile friction in plain language. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

Per the methodologist's blocking requirement, the protocol includes a pre-enactment verbalization step: after sketching and before touching the iPad, the participant fully describes their intended interaction in words. This pre-enactment description is recorded and coded as the primary framing measure, with post-enactment narration treated as secondary. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

The wizard's in-session classification sheet (reinvented / rejected / accepted-as-is) is removed from the analysis pipeline. Classification is reconstructed post-hoc from screen recordings, transcripts, and sketches by two coders blinded to which variant the wizard surfaced, with Cohen's kappa reported per idiom. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

Capture-risk audit

Probe fires sixteen named patterns across four axes — Capacity, Agency, Exit, Legibility — against every branch's prototype spec and simulated walkthrough. A pattern is only reported when the auditor can quote an evidence span from the rehearsed artifact; patterns that cannot be grounded in a specific passage are not reported. A -2 score blocks the branch.

legibility.no_failure_signal legibility score -1 branch A
source: simulated_walkthrough
The participant will show no change in behavior because the MPC continues to produce sound through its internal engine regardless of MIDI logging status... A producer who interprets 'as hard as possible' on an iPad as 'as hard as I can without damaging the screen' would produce a ceiling that is self-imposed rather than surface-imposed, and this self-imposed ceiling would be indistinguishable in the logged data from a genuine surface-imposed expressivity limit.

Two failure modes are invisible to the participant at the point of action: (1) the MPC USB dropout continues to produce audible sound so the participant receives no signal that their strikes are not being logged; (2) iOS velocity-mapping saturation is not surfaced to the participant, so deliberate dynamic variation that clips to 127 is logged as a ceiling the participant cannot perceive. The participant cannot detect system failure in-flow.

legibility.unverifiable_summary legibility score -1 branch A
source: prototype_spec
The wizard reviews the three CSV exports side by side during the interview and prepares a preliminary within-participant summary (mean velocity, velocity standard deviation, mean IOI, IOI standard deviation, and for iOS surfaces mean contact area) to inform post-session analysis but does NOT share these numbers with the participant during the session... The facilitator optionally inserts a scripted probe: 'Some producers describe a difference between what feels expressive and what registers as expressive in the data — does that distinction feel relevant to your experience today?'

The INTERVIEW_DATA_NOTE control allows the facilitator to introduce a probe grounded in an objective-data discrepancy the participant cannot see, verify, or contest. The participant is invited to reflect on an implied data pattern without any mechanism to inspect what the pattern actually is. The framing treats the wizard's preliminary summary as authoritative while withholding it, which is an unverifiable-summary dynamic inverted: the system's synthesis drives the probe but remains opaque to the person being probed.

agency.hides_options_before_review agency score -1 branch B
source: simulated_walkthrough
If a participant sketches 'a button you hold down' and then enacts a tap-and-hold on the prototype, the wizard could reasonably advance to Variant B (long-press overlay) or to Variant C (mode-switch toggle), because the participant's enactment underspecifies which they meant. The system response that the participant sees would then shape their subsequent narration

The wizard selects a single variant screen to surface in response to an ambiguous gesture without showing the participant the set of variants the selection was drawn from. The participant sees one system interpretation as if it were the interpretation, which hides the alternative framings that were available and anchors subsequent narration to the wizard's unseen choice.

legibility.no_rationale_at_point_of_action legibility score -1 branch B
source: simulated_walkthrough
A participant whose gesture triggers the NEUTRAL_SPINNER would likely experience it as a small failure — of the prototype or of themselves, depending on disposition... Under this task framing, the spinner could plausibly push verbal framing away from MOBILE_NATIVE_REFERENCE and toward HARDWARE_ANALOGY

When the wizard advances to a specific variant screen in response to an enactment, the participant sees the resulting state but is given no inline indication of why this state rather than another was surfaced. The participant cannot inspect the mapping logic (wizard classification) before narrating, so their subsequent design reasoning proceeds without knowing what the 'system' attributed to their gesture.

exit.data_lock_in exit score -1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
10 iPad Pro 12.9-inch (6th generation) devices running iOS 17, provided to participants for the study duration, each pre-enrolled in TestFlight with the instrumented app installed.

Participants are given loaner iPads with a TestFlight app and will naturalistically create musical content over four weeks, but the materials_needed and consent description mention 'TestFlight device return instructions' without any export path for patterns, sequences, or samples the participant produces. At Day 30 the device is returned and the participant's four weeks of musical work plausibly does not come with them in a portable format.

exit.institutional_dependence exit score -1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
Custom instrumented iOS sampler app (TestFlight distribution) with telemetry module logging... 10 iPad Pro 12.9-inch (6th generation) devices running iOS 17, provided to participants for the study duration, each pre-enrolled in TestFlight with the instrumented app installed.

The entire study depends on Apple's TestFlight distribution and loaned iPads, and the failure_cases section explicitly anticipates that 'the instrumented app will require at least one TestFlight update during a four-week window' — but no failure_case addresses what a participant who grew attached to the workflow does after the study ends and the custom app is no longer available through any channel they control.

legibility.no_rationale_at_point_of_action legibility score -1 branch C
source: simulated_walkthrough
A participant seeing a chart labeled 'abandonment event timeline' would reasonably read the word 'abandonment' as a claim about their own behavior and would plausibly either confirm or push back on that framing in ways that are partly a reaction to the labeling rather than to the underlying behavior.

At the exit interview, participants are asked to narrate three telemetry-derived charts (feature-use frequency, abandonment timeline, Likert trajectory) presented with researcher-chosen labels and a wizard-chosen smoothing decision, without any inline mechanism for the participant to see what operational definition 'abandonment' uses (≥5 days no access) before committing to an interpretation or correction.

legibility.unverifiable_summary legibility score -1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
Facilitator presents three telemetry-derived visualizations to the participant: (a) their own feature-use frequency chart over four weeks, (b) their abandonment event timeline, and (c) their Likert naturalness ratings plotted across four weeks. Participant is asked to narrate what they see, correct any misinterpretations, and explain the reasoning behind key inflection points.

The exit-interview charts are researcher-produced syntheses of four weeks of behavior, with wizard decisions about smoothing and operational abandonment thresholds baked in, but the protocol provides no 'show underlying sessions' or drill-down affordance for the participant to verify a given inflection point against raw daily data before narrating or correcting. The simulated walkthrough notes the 60-minute pacing further constrains the retroactive-recode path.

capacity.no_rehearsal_space capacity score 1 branch A
source: prototype_spec
Minutes 0–15: facilitator reads consent form, explains that three input surfaces will be used, demonstrates the striking protocol on a neutral 'practice surface' (a foam-topped wooden board with no sensors)... Each block is 15 minutes: 2 minutes free warm-up on the surface, 10 minutes structured striking protocol

The design explicitly provides a low-stakes rehearsal space: a foam practice board with no sensors for the protocol rehearsal, plus a 2-minute free warm-up on each surface before data collection begins. This is a scaffold for skilled action, allowing participants to acclimate without their warm-up data being treated as measurement.

capacity.no_rehearsal_space capacity score 1 branch B
source: prototype_spec
25–40 min: Exploration phase — participant is given free-play time on the Figma prototype iPad while thinking aloud; wizard surfaces interactive states on demand; no tasks are assigned.

The session structure includes an explicit 15-minute low-stakes free-exploration phase before any conflict-card task is introduced, with the facilitator saying 'There are no right or wrong moves.' This is a deliberate rehearsal space that lets participants build a gesture vocabulary and interface familiarity before consequential design work begins, which scaffolds rather than substitutes for the skilled activity of the main task.

legibility.no_failure_signal legibility score 1 branch B
source: prototype_spec
Displays a grey spinner animation centered on the prototype screen for exactly 3 seconds, then automatically returns to the base state; this is the pre-agreed signal to the facilitator to ask the participant 'What were you hoping would happen there?'

The NEUTRAL_SPINNER is an explicit in-flow failure signal: when the wizard cannot map an enacted gesture to a variant, the participant sees a visible indicator and is invited to verbalize their intended outcome. The system does not fail silently; mismatch between participant intent and wizard interpretation is surfaced at the point of action and converted into elaboration data.

capacity.no_rehearsal_space capacity score 1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
Participant installs the instrumented sampler app via TestFlight link on the provided iPad Pro, opens it for the first time during the onboarding call, and freely explores the interface for 10 minutes while the facilitator observes silently.

The protocol explicitly carves out a 10-minute low-stakes free-exploration window at onboarding plus four weeks of naturalistic home-studio use before any graded assessment. Participants can try, abandon, and reinvent gestures in their own environment without task coupling, which is a genuine rehearsal space for skill transfer.

Reviewer panel

Three Opus 4.7 agents review the surviving branch in sequence — a methodologist, an accessibility advocate, and (optionally) a novelty hawk. A meta-reviewer reads their outputs and classifies the disagreement without collapsing it to consensus.

Meta-reviewer verdict: accept_revise (consensus)

Both reviewers recommend revision rather than rejection, and their decisive concerns are non-contradictory and additive rather than opposing. The methodologist targets instrument/classifier entanglement, sample-to-claim mismatch, stimulus confounds, and scale construction; the accessibility advocate targets recruitment accessibility, consent hygiene, and compensation transparency. None of the accessibility concerns require undoing any methodologist fix, and none of the methodologist fixes foreclose the accessibility fixes — e.g., operationalizing the MPC-exclusion screener is compatible with adding an accessibility-needs item; reframing from 'design vocabulary' to case descriptions is compatible with narrowing the population claim to non-AT-using mobile-native novices. The recommendations differ by one level (major vs. minor), which falls within the consensus band, and the advocate explicitly disclaims blocking authority on this branch. Revisions should address the methodologist's blocking items (wizard classification removed from pipeline; pre-enactment verbalization added) and both reviewers' major items before the branch advances.

ReviewerRecommendationDecisive weakness
methodologist major_revision The wizard is simultaneously the measurement instrument and the classifier whose output becomes the primary finding, with no blinding, no independent coding, and no inter-rater reliability on the classification sheet that drives the headline 'design vocabulary' claim. Additionally, n=6 with a 4/6 threshold cannot support a categorical taxonomy, the hardware-video orientation confounds the 'spontaneous encounter' RQ, and the transfer-tolerance composite is an unvalidated scale used to produce categorical cutoffs.
accessibility_advocate minor_revision No decisive weakness found at the accessibility-advocate threshold (no paternalistic, extractive, or curative design failure). Remaining concerns: recruitment and prototype are silent on disability accommodation (likely default-excluding blind/low-vision/motor-disabled mobile producers whose idioms diverge most from MPC hardware), consent hygiene does not disclose cognitive load of the 5-cycle protocol, and compensation rate for generative design labor is unspecified.

What humans must decide

Every Probe guidebook ends with a [HUMAN_REQUIRED] block. This is not a closing pleasantry — it is the set of decisions the pipeline is not authorized to make on the researcher's behalf.

Before running this study, the researcher must: (1) decide and document the orientation-phase framing choice (drop the hardware video or reframe the research question) and revise all study materials to match; (2) audit the Figma prototype for assistive-technology compatibility or explicitly narrow the population claim; (3) finalize the operationalized MPC-exclusion screener items and the borderline-exposure decision rule; (4) prepare the two-coder blinded post-hoc classification protocol including the codebook and kappa reporting plan; (5) name the compensation amount and justify it against 120 minutes of generative design labor; (6) pilot the full protocol including the pre-enactment verbalization step with at least one participant who meets the screener, and revise the hand-signal vocabulary and wizard variant-selection rules based on pilot findings; (7) obtain IRB approval covering the revised consent language, break protocol, and compensation rate; (8) independently verify and ground the uncited adjacent literatures flagged in the Background section before citing them in any write-up. [HUMAN_REQUIRED]
