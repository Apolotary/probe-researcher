# Prototype: Corpus-Personalized Divergence Probe: WoZ Drafting Study with Poets

## Context

- **Setting:** A quiet single-occupancy room at a university research lab. The participant sits at a desk facing a laptop. The wizard and observer are in an immediately adjacent room connected via a one-way audio feed and a shared real-time view of the participant's drafting interface (screen-mirrored via VNC on a second laptop). A privacy screen prevents the participant from seeing the adjacent room.
- **Device:** Participant: 13-inch laptop running Chrome 124, 1440x900 resolution, with a custom web-based drafting interface served from localhost. Wizard: separate 15-inch laptop running VNC viewer and a wizard control panel (HTML page, Chrome 124) for queuing candidate sets. Both machines on the same local network; no internet required during the session.
- **Duration:** 90 minutes

0–10 min: Facilitator delivers verbal briefing, participant reads and signs consent form, facilitator explains the drafting interface using a 5-minute warm-up poem task not used in analysis. 10–15 min: Facilitator introduces CONDITION ORDER (counterbalanced across participants: half begin with the corpus-divergence tool, half with the thesaurus control) and explains Task Prompt A. 15–40 min: Participant drafts Poem A (25 min hard limit signaled by facilitator; wizard active throughout). 40–45 min: Facilitator administers 5-item post-task Likert survey and one open-ended question about Poem A. 45–50 min: Facilitator introduces condition switch and Task Prompt B. 50–75 min: Participant drafts Poem B (25 min hard limit). 75–80 min: Second post-task survey administered. 80–90 min: Semi-structured debriefing interview conducted by facilitator; wizard joins room only after debriefing begins.

## Actors

- **participant** (1): A practicing poet with at least 10 poems published in traceable venues, recruited to draft two new poems during the session using the tool under study.
- **wizard** (1): A researcher seated in an adjacent room who monitors the participant's draft in real time and manually queues the next batch of line-ending candidates to display in the interface, selecting from a pre-prepared candidate bank keyed to the participant's prior corpus.
- **observer** (1): A second researcher in the adjacent room who logs timestamped notes on wizard decisions, notes any latency spikes between the participant pressing the suggestion button and candidates appearing, and flags anomalies for post-session review.
- **facilitator** (1): A researcher present in the participant's room who delivers the briefing, manages consent, introduces the two task prompts in sequence, administers the post-task surveys, and conducts the debriefing interview.

## Task flow

### Step 1

- **Participant:** Participant reads the task prompt card displayed on screen (e.g., 'Write a poem of at least 8 lines about an object you no longer own') and begins typing the first line of the poem in the drafting textarea.
- **System:** The interface displays a blinking cursor in the textarea and an inactive 'Suggest endings' button beneath the text area. No suggestions appear until the participant explicitly requests them.
- **Wizard decisions:**
  - Confirm via VNC that the participant has typed at least a partial first line before enabling the suggestion button on the wizard control panel; do not enable early if the participant is still reading the prompt.
  - Load the correct candidate bank for this participant-condition combination (corpus-divergence bank or thesaurus bank) in the wizard control panel before the participant finishes reading.

### Step 2

- **Participant:** Participant finishes typing a line and clicks the 'Suggest endings' button to request line-ending candidates for the current line.
- **System:** After a simulated latency of 1.5–2.5 seconds (randomized by the wizard control panel to mimic API response time), a panel slides open on the right side of the interface displaying exactly 5 line-ending candidates as clickable text chips. Each chip shows only the candidate word or short phrase (2–4 words maximum), with no score, rank, or provenance label visible to the participant.
- **Wizard decisions:**
  - Select which 5 candidates from the pre-prepared candidate bank to queue for this specific line, based on the semantic content of the line the participant just typed as viewed via VNC.
  - In the corpus-divergence condition, choose candidates that are confirmed low-frequency in the participant's indexed prior corpus AND phonologically distinct from the current line's dominant stress pattern; in the thesaurus condition, select the top 5 synonyms for the most semantically prominent word in the line from the pre-loaded thesaurus lookup table.
  - Decide whether the 1.5–2.5 second latency window is sufficient to complete candidate selection; if not, extend by triggering the 'extend_latency' control before the panel opens.

### Step 3

- **Participant:** Participant reviews the 5 candidate chips and either clicks one to insert it as the line ending (accept event) or dismisses the panel by clicking an 'X' button and continues typing their own ending (reject event).
- **System:** On accept: the chosen candidate is appended to the current line in the textarea, the suggestion panel closes, and the interface logs an accept event with a timestamp, the candidate text, and the line index. On reject: the panel closes, no text is inserted, and the interface logs a reject event with timestamp and line index. In both cases the 'Suggest endings' button becomes active again for the next line.
- **Wizard decisions:**
  - Verify via the observer's log that the accept or reject event fired correctly in the interface log (visible in a side panel on the wizard laptop); if the log entry is missing, trigger the 'manual_log_entry' control within 5 seconds.
  - Note in the wizard's own timestamped decision log which candidate was selected and whether it was the highest-divergence or lowest-divergence option among the 5 queued, for post-session cross-referencing.

### Step 4

- **Participant:** Participant continues drafting, repeating the request-review-accept/reject cycle across multiple lines until they declare the poem finished or the 25-minute limit is reached. If the participant pauses for more than 90 seconds without typing, the facilitator prompts them with 'Take as long as you need; just keep drafting.'
- **System:** The interface accumulates the full draft in the textarea, logs each suggestion request event, and displays a passive word count in the lower right corner. No other feedback (e.g., readability score, rhyme indicator) is shown. A subtle gray progress bar in the lower left shows elapsed time without a numeric countdown.
- **Wizard decisions:**
  - Monitor the participant's request rate; if the participant has not clicked 'Suggest endings' in the last 5 minutes and appears to be drafting freely, do not intervene.
  - If the participant requests suggestions for the same line twice (edits a line and re-requests), re-select 5 candidates appropriate to the revised line content, ensuring no overlap with the first set of 5 candidates shown for that line.
  - If the participant appears to be copying a candidate verbatim and then requesting suggestions again for a downstream line that echoes the inserted candidate's phonology, flag this in the wizard decision log as a 'phonological entrainment' event for post-session analysis.

### Step 5

- **Participant:** Participant clicks 'Finish poem' button or the facilitator signals the 25-minute limit aloud. Participant then completes the post-task survey on a paper form handed by the facilitator.
- **System:** The interface locks the textarea (makes it read-only), displays a confirmation message ('Draft saved'), and exports a timestamped JSON log file containing the full draft text, all suggestion events (accepted and rejected), candidate texts presented, and line-level timestamps. The facilitator retrieves the log file from the participant laptop's Downloads folder immediately after the lock.
- **Wizard decisions:**
  - Confirm the JSON log export completed by checking the file size in the Downloads folder via VNC; if the file is 0 bytes or missing, trigger 'manual_export_fallback' control before the participant begins the survey.
  - Annotate the wizard decision log with a final count of: total suggestion requests, total accepts, total rejects, and any latency extension events triggered during the session.

## Wizard controls

- **queue_candidate_set** — triggers: Wizard fires this control within 1.5 seconds of observing the participant click the 'Suggest endings' button via VNC. The wizard selects 5 candidate entries from the active condition's bank and clicks 'Queue & Release' in the wizard control panel.. Effect: The selected 5 candidate texts are pushed via a localhost WebSocket to the participant's interface, where they appear as text chips in the suggestion panel after the randomized latency delay (1.5–2.5 s). The control panel clears the selection fields and readies for the next request.
- **extend_latency** — triggers: Wizard fires this control when the 1.5-second default preparation window is insufficient — typically when the participant's line content requires the wizard to search a secondary lexical sub-bank not yet open on the wizard laptop. Must be triggered before the default 1.5 s elapses.. Effect: Extends the loading spinner on the participant's interface by an additional 2 seconds (total latency becomes 3.5–4.5 s). A status indicator on the wizard panel turns yellow to confirm extension is active. The wizard then completes candidate selection and fires 'queue_candidate_set' normally.
- **manual_log_entry** — triggers: Wizard fires this control when the observer confirms that an accept or reject event did not register in the interface log (visible in the session log panel on the wizard laptop) within 5 seconds of the participant's interaction.. Effect: Injects a manually entered log record into the session JSON log via a form on the wizard control panel, including fields for event type (accept/reject), candidate text if applicable, line index, and a flag marking the entry as 'wizard_manual' rather than 'system_auto'. This flag is preserved in the exported JSON for post-session exclusion or sensitivity analysis.
- **manual_export_fallback** — triggers: Wizard fires this control at Step 5 if the automatic JSON export produces a 0-byte file or fails to appear in the Downloads folder within 10 seconds of the participant clicking 'Finish poem'.. Effect: Triggers a secondary export routine on the participant laptop (a bookmarklet pre-installed in Chrome) that serializes the in-memory session state to a JSON file named with a '-fallback' suffix. The wizard instructs the facilitator via a silent text message on a shared phone to check the Downloads folder for the fallback file.

## Observable signals

- **Accept/reject event stream with candidate texts, line indices, and timestamps for each suggestion request across both conditions** (log_events) — For each accepted candidate in the corpus-divergence condition, compute a divergence score as (1 - cosine similarity) between the candidate's lemma vector and the centroid of the participant's prior corpus vocabulary, using a pre-built TF-IDF index of each poet's indexed prior work. Compute the same score for accepted thesaurus candidates. A paired Wilcoxon signed-rank test (one per participant, then aggregated) will compare median divergence scores across conditions. Acceptance rate (accepts / total requests) will be computed per condition per participant to assess whether poets engage differently with the two candidate types. Line-index patterns will be inspected to assess whether accepts cluster at specific structural positions (e.g., final couplet lines).
- **Participant verbal commentary on candidate choices during drafting (spontaneous, not prompted)** (think_aloud_audio) — Transcribe audio for each session. Apply a pre-specified 3-category coding scheme to each utterance that references a suggestion: (a) habitualness comment — participant notes the candidate matches or conflicts with their usual style (e.g., 'I would never use that word'), (b) pragmatic comment — participant evaluates fit with the current line's meaning or sound without reference to personal style, (c) no comment — candidate used or rejected silently. Inter-rater reliability will be computed on 20% of utterances by a second coder blind to condition. The ratio of habitualness-to-pragmatic comments will be compared across conditions to assess whether corpus-divergence candidates elicit more explicit style-awareness.
- **Post-task self-report: perceived stylistic distance of the finished poem from the participant's usual work** (self_report_likert) — A 5-item Likert battery (7-point agree/disagree) administered after each poem covers: (1) 'This poem sounds like my usual work', (2) 'The suggestions pushed me toward words I would not have chosen', (3) 'I felt in control of the word choices', (4) 'The suggestions were useful', (5) 'This poem surprised me'. Items 1 and 3 are reverse-scored. A composite 'perceived divergence' score (items 1+2+5, reverse-scoring item 1) will be computed per poem per participant and compared across conditions using a Wilcoxon signed-rank test. Item 3 serves as a manipulation check for perceived autonomy.
- **Full draft text of each poem, exported as plain text for computational prosodic and lexical analysis** (screen_recording) — Screen recordings provide a timestamped visual backup of the full draft evolution for cases where log export fails. Post-session, extract the final draft text from the recording if the JSON export is missing or flagged as corrupted. The final draft text (from either source) will be analyzed using the CMU Pronouncing Dictionary to compute a prosodic divergence score: the proportion of stressed syllables in line-ending positions that deviate from the dominant stress pattern in the participant's prior corpus (measured as the modal stress pattern across line endings in their indexed work). Lexical divergence will be computed as the proportion of content words in the finished poem not appearing in the participant's prior corpus top-2000 vocabulary.
- **Wizard decision latency: time in seconds between the participant clicking 'Suggest endings' and the wizard firing 'queue_candidate_set'** (log_events) — The wizard control panel logs a timestamp when 'queue_candidate_set' is fired; this is subtracted from the suggestion-button click timestamp in the interface log to compute wizard decision latency per request. Requests with latency > 3.5 s (indicating a latency extension event was triggered) will be flagged. Median wizard decision latency will be reported per session as a fidelity metric. Sessions where more than 20% of requests required latency extension will be reviewed for wizard preparation failures and may be excluded from the divergence score analysis pending team review.
- **Post-session open-ended debriefing response: participant's account of how they decided to accept or reject suggestions** (self_report_open_ended) — Facilitator asks three fixed questions: (1) 'Walk me through how you decided whether to use a suggestion or not', (2) 'Did the suggestions in either session feel more or less like your own voice?', (3) 'Was there a moment when a suggestion changed the direction of the poem?' Responses are transcribed. Each response is coded for presence or absence of three pre-specified themes: (a) corpus-self-comparison (participant invokes their prior work as a reference), (b) sound/prosody reasoning, (c) semantic/meaning reasoning. Frequencies of theme occurrence will be tallied per condition-reference in the debriefing and reported descriptively alongside the quantitative divergence measures.

## Failure cases

- **The wizard cannot identify 5 corpus-divergence candidates for a line because the participant's line content falls in a semantic domain with no low-frequency counterparts in the pre-built candidate bank (e.g., the participant writes a highly idiosyncratic proper noun or invented word as the line's pivot).** — expected: The wizard should still deliver exactly 5 candidates within the allotted latency window; delivering fewer or visibly stalling would break the interface illusion and alert the participant to the WoZ setup.. Wizard fallback: The wizard fires 'extend_latency' to buy an extra 2 seconds, then selects the 5 lowest-frequency candidates in the bank regardless of semantic proximity to the line, and annotates the wizard decision log with 'domain_gap_fallback' for that event. These events will be flagged in post-session analysis and excluded from the corpus-divergence scoring calculation, with the exclusion reported in the methods.
- **The participant begins editing previously typed lines rather than advancing to new lines, causing the suggestion panel to be invoked for a line the wizard has already prepared candidates for, but whose content has now changed substantially (e.g., 6 words replaced out of 8).** — expected: The interface should present 5 fresh candidates appropriate to the revised line, not the original 5; re-showing identical candidates after substantial revision would create a noticeable mismatch that prompts participant suspicion.. Wizard fallback: The wizard fires 'extend_latency' immediately upon detecting the re-request via VNC, uses the extra time to select 5 new candidates from the bank with no overlap with the prior set shown for that line index, and logs a 'line_revision_re-request' event in the wizard decision log. If the participant has made only minor edits (1–2 words changed), the wizard may re-queue the original 5 candidates minus any that directly echoed the now-deleted words.
- **The automatic JSON log export fails at Step 5 (0-byte file or missing file), AND the manual_export_fallback bookmarklet also fails because the participant navigated away from the drafting page before export completed.** — expected: The session data would be lost, making the participant's data unanalyzable for the primary divergence measures.. Wizard fallback: The observer, who has been watching the screen via VNC throughout, immediately saves a full-resolution screen recording segment covering the entire drafting period using the observer laptop's local recording software (OBS, configured to record the VNC window continuously). The facilitator asks the participant to remain seated while the wizard enters the room and attempts to recover the in-memory session state by re-opening the browser's developer console and copying the sessionStorage JSON manually. If recovery takes more than 5 minutes, the session is logged as a partial-data session and excluded from primary analysis, with the screen recording retained as a fallback source for draft text only.

## Materials needed

- Custom web-based drafting interface: a single HTML/JS/CSS page (served via localhost Python HTTP server) with a textarea, 'Suggest endings' button, suggestion panel with 5 chip slots, 'Finish poem' button, passive word count display, and elapsed-time progress bar. Interface must emit WebSocket events to the wizard control panel on button clicks and receive candidate arrays via the same WebSocket.
- Wizard control panel: a separate HTML/JS page served on the same localhost, showing a live mirror of the participant's textarea content (pulled via WebSocket), 5 text input fields for candidate entry, a 'Queue & Release' button, an 'Extend Latency' button, a 'Manual Log Entry' form, and a running session event log with timestamps.
- VNC server installed on participant laptop (e.g., TigerVNC); VNC viewer configured on wizard laptop to display participant screen at full resolution with read-only permissions.
- Pre-built corpus-divergence candidate bank: for each enrolled participant, a JSON file keyed by semantic domain cluster (minimum 20 clusters, 30 candidates each) containing candidate words and 2–4 word phrases confirmed to be low-frequency (below the 10th percentile) in that participant's indexed prior work and phonologically coded by stress pattern using CMU Pronouncing Dictionary.
- Pre-built thesaurus candidate bank: a single JSON file containing synonym lists for 500 high-frequency English content words, drawn from WordNet 3.1, used as the control condition candidate source for all participants.
- TF-IDF index of each participant's prior published work: Python pickle files (scikit-learn TfidfVectorizer) built from plain-text files of each poet's traceable published poems, used for post-session divergence scoring.
- Two task prompt cards (displayed on-screen as text overlays): Prompt A — 'Write a poem of at least 8 lines about an object you no longer own'; Prompt B — 'Write a poem of at least 8 lines about a sound you associate with a specific time of year.' Prompts were pretested with 3 pilot poets not in the study sample to confirm they do not strongly favor particular lexical registers.
- Counterbalancing assignment sheet: a pre-generated table assigning each participant ID to a condition order (corpus-divergence first vs. thesaurus first) and prompt order (Prompt A first vs. Prompt B first), balanced using a 2x2 Latin square.
- 5-item post-task Likert survey on paper (two copies per session, one per poem), printed in 14pt font with a 7-point agree/disagree scale.
- Facilitator debriefing script: a printed A4 sheet with the three fixed debriefing questions and instructions for probe follow-up questions (e.g., 'Can you give me an example?' after vague answers), with a note to avoid mentioning the corpus-personalization mechanism until after the participant has answered all three questions.
- OBS Studio configured on observer laptop to record the VNC window continuously throughout the session at 1080p, saved to a local folder named with participant ID and session date.
- Consent form and demographic questionnaire (one page): collects name, publication venues for prior work (used to build the corpus index), years writing poetry, and confirmation that the participant holds rights to share their prior published text for corpus analysis.
- Shared silent-communication phone (one per session): a spare smartphone with iMessage or Signal open between facilitator and wizard, used for silent coordination (e.g., 'log export failed, triggering fallback') without disrupting the participant.
- Pilot-tested timing sheet for the facilitator: a printed schedule with clock times filled in at session start, marking each phase transition (briefing end, poem 1 start, poem 1 hard stop, survey 1, poem 2 start, poem 2 hard stop, survey 2, debrief start).
