Capture-risk audit

Probe fires sixteen named patterns across four axes — Capacity, Agency, Exit, Legibility — against every branch's prototype spec and simulated walkthrough. A pattern is only reported when the auditor can quote an evidence span from the rehearsed artifact; patterns that cannot be grounded in a specific passage are not reported. A -2 score blocks the branch.

agency.hides_options_before_review agency score -1 branch B
source: prototype_spec
the wizard injects the pre-authored high-credibility fixture HTML: a 180-word answer paragraph containing two inline superscript citation markers ([1] and [2]) hyperlinked to nih.gov and mayoclinic.org respectively

Each trial presents a single pre-authored answer with exactly two pre-selected citation sources. The participant has no visibility into why these two sources were selected, what other sources might have been retrieved, or what the full candidate set looked like. The simulated RAG system presents one fixed output as if it were the result of a live retrieval process, hiding the selection space entirely. While this is methodologically necessary for the WoZ design, it structurally instantiates the hide-options pattern on every trial.

legibility.no_failure_signal legibility score -1 branch B
source: prototype_spec
the answer text for matched high/low pairs covers the same factual claims so that content cannot serve as a credibility signal

The study is specifically designed so that answer content provides no signal that the RAG system has retrieved low-quality sources. When a low-credibility fixture is injected — for example, citing vaccinedangers.org or coinconspiracy.info — the answer body makes identical factual claims to the high-credibility version, meaning the content itself cannot alert the participant that the system's source selection has failed. The system fails silently on credibility, with no in-flow indicator that the retrieval has gone wrong. This is the core ecological condition the study is designed to probe, but it structurally instantiates the no-failure-signal pattern for every low-credibility trial.

source: prototype_spec
After a simulated 2.5-second processing delay (implemented as a CSS spinner on the page), the wizard injects the pre-authored high-credibility fixture HTML: a 180-word answer paragraph containing two inline superscript citation markers ([1] and [2]) hyperlinked to nih.gov and mayoclinic.org respectively; citation reference entries appear below the answer in a styled reference list showing domain name, article title, and publication year.

The simulated RAG system provides no inline rationale at any point explaining why these two sources were selected, what retrieval criteria were applied, or what confidence level underlies the answer. The participant receives a completed answer with citations but no mechanism to inspect the selection logic before deciding whether to trust or verify the answer. The only potentially legible signal is the domain name itself, which the study treats as the credibility cue under investigation — meaning the system's source-selection rationale is structurally absent by design.

legibility.unverifiable_summary legibility score -1 branch B
source: prototype_spec
a 180-word answer paragraph containing two inline superscript citation markers ([1] and [2]) hyperlinked to nih.gov and mayoclinic.org respectively; citation reference entries appear below the answer in a styled reference list showing domain name, article title, and publication year

The 180-word answer paragraphs are pre-authored syntheses whose claims participants cannot verify against the cited source material during the session. While citation hyperlinks are present, clicking them opens pre-cached mirror pages of domain landing pages — not the specific articles that would substantiate the answer claims. The reference list shows domain name, article title, and publication year, but no mechanism exists for the participant to trace a specific factual claim in the answer body back to a specific passage in the cited source. The summary is structurally presented as authoritative output without a claim-level verification path.

capacity.substitutes_for_practice capacity score -1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
After a simulated 2–4 second delay (wizard triggers the response via admin panel), the interface renders a 150–200 word RAG-style answer with 4–6 inline superscript citation numbers.

The system produces the complete synthesized answer and citation mapping on the participant's behalf; the participant's role is to evaluate the output rather than to construct the factual synthesis themselves. However, critical evaluation of citations — not unaided factual recall — is explicitly the skill under study here, and the attestation widget requires the participant to perform that evaluative skill actively. The substitution concern is real but mild: the RAG answer forecloses the information-gathering act, meaning participants never practice query decomposition or source triangulation unaided. Because the study targets calibration behavior rather than information retrieval competence per se, the substitution is partially but not entirely co-extensive with the study's independent variable.

capacity.no_fade_mechanism capacity score -1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
wizard_controls: ['Inject Answer Variant', 'UI Reset', 'Log Recovery', 'Suspicion Probe Prompt']

The wizard controls catalogue lists four control types; none describes a mechanism to reduce the level of attestation scaffolding across the session as participant familiarity grows. The attestation widget, progress indicator, color-coded feedback, and gated 'Continue' button are always-on across all three tasks at identical intensity. There is no described condition where scaffolding is thinned or where the participant is invited to proceed without the widget. This fixed-intensity design is appropriate for a WoZ calibration study, but no fade mechanism exists.

capacity.no_rehearsal_space capacity score -1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
Minutes 0–10: Facilitator welcomes the participant, reads the consent form aloud, collects signature, and explains the think-aloud protocol with a 3-minute warm-up task on a neutral topic (history of the pencil) to calibrate the participant to speaking their thoughts.

The only pre-task activity described is a think-aloud calibration exercise on the pencil topic, which targets verbalization comfort, not citation evaluation skill. There is no low-stakes practice round where the participant uses the attestation widget on a dummy answer without the data being recorded. All three task instances contribute to the study's dependent variables from the first attestation interaction onward. Participants who have never evaluated RAG citations must learn to use the scaffold under full data-collection conditions with no rehearsal of the evaluative skill itself.

agency.hides_options_before_review agency score -1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
the interface renders a 150–200 word RAG-style answer with 4–6 inline superscript citation numbers. Each citation number is clickable and expands an inline popover showing: source title, author(s), publication year, a 2-sentence excerpt from the source, and a URL slug (non-functional, styled to look real).

The participant receives a single pre-authored answer variant; no alternative answers, alternative source sets, or alternative framings of the retrieved information are shown or available on request. The wizard selects among four variants but this selection is invisible to the participant, who has no mechanism to request a different answer framing, a broader set of sources, or a competing interpretation. Agency over the information space presented is fully pre-determined before the participant first sees the screen.

legibility.no_failure_signal legibility score 1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
Each row shows the cited claim as a short bolded sentence extracted from the answer, then two radio buttons labeled 'Accept' and 'Flag', then a small text field labeled 'Note (optional)'.

Counterintuitively, the attestation widget is itself a designed mechanism for participants to signal when they believe the system output may be wrong — the 'Flag' option is a user-facing failure signal built into the point-of-action flow. This directly addresses the pattern's concern that systems fail silently. The study also deliberately injects inaccurate variants (Variants B, C, D) and measures whether participants detect and flag them, making error detectability an explicit observable signal rather than an unmonitored risk. The design affirmatively scaffolds the participant's ability to surface system failures at the moment of interaction.

legibility.no_rationale_at_point_of_action legibility score 1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
Each citation number is clickable and expands an inline popover showing: source title, author(s), publication year, a 2-sentence excerpt from the source, and a URL slug (non-functional, styled to look real).

The inline citation popover is explicitly designed to provide source rationale at the point of the attestation decision — before the participant commits to Accept or Flag for each claim. The participant can inspect author, year, venue, and a two-sentence excerpt from the underlying source without leaving the decision context. This directly instantiates the pattern's positive condition: rationale is accessible inline before commitment, not deferred to a settings panel or post-hoc explanation. The design is a deliberate scaffold for rationale-at-point-of-action.

legibility.unverifiable_summary legibility score 2 branch C
source: prototype_spec
Each citation number is clickable and expands an inline popover showing: source title, author(s), publication year, a 2-sentence excerpt from the source, and a URL slug (non-functional, styled to look real). Below the answer body, the citation-attestation widget renders as a vertical list of rows, one per citation. Each row shows the cited claim as a short bolded sentence extracted from the answer.

The entire design of this prototype is specifically architected to counteract the unverifiable-summary problem. The RAG answer is decomposed into per-claim rows in the attestation widget, each linked to a clickable popover exposing the source excerpt before the participant makes any acceptance decision. The claim decomposition, inline citation linkage, and mandatory per-claim evaluation collectively constitute a multi-layered verification scaffold. This is not merely a 'show sources' button appended after the fact — the source inspection affordance is embedded in the primary decision flow at every claim. This design specifically cultivates the participant's ability to trace summary claims back to underlying sources.
