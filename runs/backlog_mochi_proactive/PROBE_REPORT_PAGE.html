Premise

The researcher's starting question asked when an AI assistant's proactive reminders feel supportive versus paternalistic in ADHD-adjacent knowledge worker contexts. [RESEARCHER_INPUT]

Sharpening surfaced that "ADHD-adjacent" was doing ambiguous work — recruitment criterion, design target, or hedge against committing to a clinical population — and that the supportive/paternalistic dichotomy is a researcher-imposed frame worth decomposing rather than accepting. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

This guidebook plans a formative elicitation study whose purpose is to recover the dimensions adults scoring at or above the ASRS v1.1 threshold actually use when sorting reminder vignettes, without being primed on the supportive/paternalistic dichotomy. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

The study can support the generation of a candidate taxonomy of participant-named dimensions to be carried into a subsequent evaluative study. [DO_NOT_CLAIM]

The study cannot support claims that any recovered dimension predicts acceptability, generalizes to adults with ADHD as a diagnostic population, or represents a population-level pattern across diagnosis status, medication status, gender, or comorbidity. [DO_NOT_CLAIM]

Background

Gaver, Dunne, and Pacenti framed probes as instruments for inspiration and dialogue, embracing uncertainty and partial returns rather than seeking comprehensive coverage of user behavior. [SOURCE_CARD:gaver_dunne_pacenti_1999]

Sanders and Stappers positioned probes alongside generative toolkits and prototypes, arguing that probe-derived material is for inspiration rather than for extracting generalizable user data — a posture this guidebook adopts by treating card-sort output as candidate vocabulary, not as measured effects. [SOURCE_CARD:sanders_stappers_2014]

Buijsman, Carter, and Bermúdez distinguish domain-specific autonomy as having two components — skilled competence and authentic value-formation — and argue that AI decision-support can erode these via absent failure indicators and unconscious value shifts; this frames why "supportive vs. controlling" is too coarse for the folk dichotomy the study is trying to decompose. [SOURCE_CARD:buijsman_carter_bermudez_2025]

Longoni and colleagues identify "uniqueness neglect" — the belief that AI cannot account for consumers' unique characteristics — as a primary driver of resistance to AI in consequential domains, suggesting that perceived fit between the system's model of the user and the user's self-model is a candidate dimension to watch for in sorting behavior. [SOURCE_CARD:longoni_2019_medical_ai_resistance]

Illich's convivial/industrial tool distinction — whether a tool expands or contracts the range of autonomous action — offers a theoretical anchor for why reversibility and user-initiated goal-setting may surface as participant-named dimensions rather than as researcher-imposed ones. [SOURCE_CARD:illich_1973]

Named outside-corpus literature the reviewer flagged which the researcher must verify and ground before citing: the Mark, Iqbal, and Czerwinski lineage on notification interruption and acceptability; Calvo and Peters on wellbeing-supportive and autonomy-supportive design in behavior-change apps; Deci and Ryan's Basic Psychological Needs Scale as the source of the 5-item autonomy-perception adaptation; Nielsen's and Tullis & Wood's published card-sort sample-size guidance as comparators for the N=12 justification. [UNCITED_ADJACENT]

Method

Probe does not run a study. It writes one, rehearses it, and audits the rehearsal. This section restates the prototype specification — the artifact a different researcher could build from without asking clarifying questions — and the study protocol.

The prototype is a single 90-minute in-person or remote session in which one participant sorts 40 reminder-scenario vignette cards into participant-constructed piles while thinking aloud, supported by a facilitator, a wizard operating the card-display and logging interface, and a silent observer taking timestamped non-verbal notes. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

The 40 vignettes are constructed on a 4-variable fractional factorial (resolution IV minimum) varying timing (immediate / +15 min / +2 hr), framing (informational / directive / collaborative), source of goal (user-initiated / system-inferred / third-party), and reversibility (dismissible / snooze-only / not-dismissible). [AGENT_INFERENCE]

The session opens with consent, then a 3-card warm-up on an unrelated topic (weather) to rehearse the think-aloud protocol before the reminder-sorting phase begins. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

Core sorting (cards 1–40) proceeds at participant-directed pace with a silence-prompt every 90 seconds; the wizard advances cards only after the participant has finished reading, speaking, and dragging. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

After all cards are placed, the participant reviews each pile, assigns a label in their own words, and provides a 2–3 sentence rationale; the wizard captures the final state as a screenshot and as a JSON export. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

A 10-minute semi-structured debrief covers the hardest-to-place card, the single most important criterion the participant used, and any reminder type they feel belongs in none of their piles. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

A 5-item autonomy-perception questionnaire adapted from the Basic Psychological Needs Scale autonomy subscale is administered at session end, referencing the reminders seen in the study rather than general life contexts. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

Data capture comprises think-aloud audio (transcribed), card-placement event logs (Google Sheet and SQLite fallback), pile labels and rationales, observer non-verbal notes, a final-state screenshot and JSON export, and the autonomy-perception questionnaire. [AGENT_INFERENCE]

Capture-risk audit

Probe fires sixteen named patterns across four axes — Capacity, Agency, Exit, Legibility — against every branch's prototype spec and simulated walkthrough. A pattern is only reported when the auditor can quote an evidence span from the rehearsed artifact; patterns that cannot be grounded in a specific passage are not reported. A -2 score blocks the branch.

legibility.no_failure_signal legibility score -2 branch C
source: simulated_walkthrough
by week 3 or 4 a reasonably attentive participant would likely perceive the '3 days' claim in archetype C as no longer corresponding to their actual study behavior — and whether they code that perception as 'system is non-adaptive' or 'my own study pattern has diverged' is itself the construct the study is trying to measure.

The reminders make factual claims ('47 cards due', '68% retention', '3 days since review') that are known to diverge from reality as weeks progress, and the system delivers these as statements of fact with no in-flow signal that the numbers are frozen week-1 snapshots. Participants cannot distinguish system-is-stale from my-behavior-changed. The failure mode is structurally invisible at the moment of action — a participant receiving 'your retention has dropped to 68%' in week 5 has no surface telling them this figure is hardcoded.

agency.weak_override agency score -1 branch A
source: simulated_walkthrough
The wizard's option to type a verbal label into the field on the participant's behalf if they haven't touched the input within 15 seconds is a pacing convenience that carries methodological cost. A participant hearing their own phrase typed back at them on screen would plausibly treat it as a commitment rather than a draft

The protocol allows the wizard to type a label into the participant's pile-label field on their behalf after 15 seconds of inactivity. The participant can technically edit this later, but the label is already committed to screen and framed as their words; the walkthrough identifies this as a commitment-framing effect. The override path (editing the wizard's entry) is more effortful and socially awkward than accepting the pre-typed label.

agency.paternalistic_default agency score -1 branch A
source: prototype_spec
Decide whether the pacing requires inserting a 2-minute rest break (participant shows signs of fatigue: monosyllabic responses, long silences, repeated sighing) before card 30; if so, signal the facilitator via back-channel chat with the message 'REST NOW'.

Participants are an ASRS-screened high-symptom population (a group historically subject to paternalistic assumptions about executive function). The protocol makes the fatigue-break decision on the participant's behalf via wizard observation of behavioral cues rather than asking the participant if they want a break. The walkthrough also flags that the criteria would miss masked-fatigue cases in participants actively managing self-presentation — exactly the population the default is aimed at. There is no described opt-in or self-request channel for breaks.

legibility.no_failure_signal legibility score -1 branch B
source: simulated_walkthrough
In the system-inferred condition, a participant reading the paraphrased version ('correspondence tasks' instead of 'client emails') would likely notice the category-label substitution, and the reaction would plausibly depend on whether the participant interprets the abstraction as intelligent generalization or as imprecise guessing. The fixed paraphrase rule is operationally clean but produces a single paraphrase style per participant

In the system-inferred condition the reminder can be subtly wrong (paraphrase drifts from intent), and the participant has no in-flow surface to correct, flag, or see why the paraphrase was chosen. The walkthrough explicitly notes this accuracy ambiguity, and observable_signals capture participant reactions post-hoc rather than letting the participant signal error at point of action. Accuracy perception is only surfaced in open-ended items after the block ends.

legibility.no_rationale_at_point_of_action legibility score -1 branch B
source: prototype_spec
Sends a toast notification reading: 'Based on your activity patterns, it looks like you were working on [system-generated paraphrase of the goal for this block] — want to return?'

The system-inferred toast invokes 'activity patterns' as the basis for the reminder but provides no inline mechanism for the participant to inspect what those patterns actually are before clicking 'Back to work' or 'Not now'. The rationale is gestured at rhetorically but not inspectable at the point of decision, which is precisely the legibility gap the pattern targets.

capacity.no_fade_mechanism capacity score -1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
four reminder archetypes per week at pre-specified times (Monday 09:00, Tuesday 20:00, Thursday 12:00, Saturday 17:00 local time), each identical in wording and trigger logic across all six weeks

The reminder regime is delivered at fixed intensity for six weeks with no mechanism for the participant to reduce cadence as their own study habit strengthens. A proactive reminder system being prototyped this way could cultivate reliance on external prompting rather than internal initiation, and the design provides no conditional gating on user self-signal.

agency.paternalistic_default agency score -1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
'You haven't reviewed in 3 days — your recall on recent material is fading'

The reminder archetypes include explicitly scolding/warning language aimed at lapsed users, and the study deliberately recruits 'lapsed users' as a subgroup. Archetype C frames the user's behavior as a failure state and archetype A invokes streak-preservation pressure. These defaults assume the participant wants to be pushed toward daily review, without evidence that lapsed users endorse that identity; the walkthrough itself notes archetype C will be factually wrong for the most lapsed participants.

exit.institutional_dependence exit score -1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
Read-only Mochi account access credentials (shared per participant) for the wizard to pull week-1 deck statistics for archetype personalization.

The study requires participants to share Mochi account credentials with researchers for data extraction, creating an institutional dependency during the study period. No failure case addresses what happens if Mochi changes its terms, if Firebase access is disrupted, or how participants revoke the shared credential access post-study. The consent and credential-revocation flow on exit is underspecified.

legibility.no_rationale_at_point_of_action legibility score -1 branch C

'Based on your usual pattern, evenings work well for you — 15 cards before bed?'

Archetype D asserts a personalized rationale ('based on your usual pattern') with no inline mechanism for the participant to inspect what pattern the system is claiming to detect or why evenings were selected. The reminder presents itself as reasoning from user data when no such reasoning occurred. At the point of action (tap/dismiss), the participant has no inspection surface for the claim.

legibility.unverifiable_summary legibility score -1 branch C

Archetype B delivers a quantitative summary of the participant's retention performance with no affordance to trace the figure back to Mochi's actual data. The participant cannot verify '68%' against their source before deciding whether to review. 0–5 min: Facilitator reads standardized welcome script, obtains verbal consent to audio/video recording, explains think-aloud protocol using a 3-card warm-up sort on unrelated topic (types of weather).

The session_structure explicitly includes a low-stakes 3-card warm-up on an unrelated topic (weather) to rehearse the think-aloud protocol before the task-coupled phase begins. This is a deliberate rehearsal space for the skill being asked of the participant (concurrent verbalization during sorting), satisfying the pattern's positive form.

legibility.no_failure_signal legibility score 1 branch A
source: prototype_spec
The wizard's workstation runs a local Node.js log-relay script (log_relay.js) that writes every drag-end event to a local SQLite file independently of the WebSocket connection, as a fallback log. If disconnection is detected (wizard sees 'Connection lost' banner in their admin panel), the wizard immediately sends 'PAUSE' via back-channel chat.

The failure_cases section explicitly addresses system-failure scenarios (WebSocket disconnection, browser crash) with visible signals ('Connection lost' banner) and a protocol response (pause, photo-recovery, manual re-entry). The system does not fail silently; the wizard sees the failure and triggers a recovery script. This is the positive form of the pattern — failures are made visible.

source: prototype_spec
Minutes 15–20: Practice block with a fourth goal not used in test conditions; wizard dispatches one practice reminder (always user-stated type) so participant experiences the reminder UI before data collection begins.

The session structure explicitly includes a low-stakes practice block with a non-test goal where the participant rehearses the reminder UI before measured blocks begin. This is a deliberate rehearsal affordance that scaffolds familiarity with the interaction without contaminating test conditions, meriting a positive scaffold score.

agency.weak_override agency score 1 branch C
source: prototype_spec
Participant taps, dismisses, or ignores each notification as they see fit... Dismiss and ignore are both logged as distinct events.

Override here is strong and frictionless: the participant can dismiss or simply ignore any notification with zero penalty, and both paths are logged as first-class events rather than treated as deviance. There is no persuasive re-prompt, no nagging escalation, and no framed-as-deviant override path. This is a scaffold for agency, not erosion.

Reviewer panel

Three Opus 4.7 agents review the surviving branch in sequence — a methodologist, an accessibility advocate, and (optionally) a novelty hawk. A meta-reviewer reads their outputs and classifies the disagreement without collapsing it to consensus.

Meta-reviewer verdict: human_judgment_required (legitimate_methodological_split)

Both reviewers land at major_revision, which would suggest consensus on disposition, but their blocking-severity objections target different and non-overlapping defects that cannot be resolved by a single revision pass. The methodologist's blocking concerns are internal-validity failures: no DV for the predictive claim, and an analysis threshold/clustering pipeline that cannot support the dimension-recovery claim at N=12. The accessibility advocate's blocking concerns are constitutive: the instrument itself is miscalibrated because the target population did not co-design it, and the session's authority asymmetries (wizard-controlled breaks, wizard-typed labels) will distort the very data the methodologist wants cleaner. Fixing the methodologist's DV problem by adding acceptability Likerts does not address co-design absence or paternalistic defaults; fixing the accessibility advocate's co-design and participant-agency problems may reshape the vignette corpus and session structure in ways that invalidate the current fractional factorial and N=12 justification. The two critiques interact: co-design could expose dimensions not varied in the corpus (reinforcing the methodologist's confound point), and removing the wizard-types rule changes the behavioral log data feeding the co-occurrence clustering. Sequencing and scope of revision — whether to run co-design first and then rebuild the methodology, or to lock methodology and bolt on accommodations — is a research-design decision the agents cannot adjudicate.

methodologist major_revision The central predictive claim ('participant-named dimensions predict acceptability better than valence of framing alone') has no measured DV in the protocol — the card-sort never elicits per-vignette acceptability. Compounded by N=12 with a ≥50% threshold vulnerable to vocabulary fragmentation, a fractional-factorial vignette corpus that structurally constrains which dimensions the behavioral clustering can recover, and a median-split re-clustering the walkthrough itself flags as underpowered.
accessibility_advocate major_revision ADHD adults appear only as subjects, not as co-designers of the instrument intended to expose the inadequacy of a researcher-constructed frame. What humans must decide

Every Probe guidebook ends with a [HUMAN_REQUIRED] block. This is not a closing pleasantry — it is the set of decisions the pipeline is not authorized to make on the researcher's behalf.

Before running this study, the researcher must: (1) decide the sequencing question between co-design-first (which may reshape corpus, session structure, and sample size justification) versus methodology-lock-then-accommodate (which preserves the current analytic plan but leaves the instrument miscalibrated); (2) decide whether the contribution is descriptive (drop predictive language from the claim) or predictive (add a per-vignette acceptability DV and rebuild the analysis around it); (3) run at least 3 paid co-design sessions with ADHD adults and document changes; (4) obtain IRB approval for the compensation structure, accommodation options, and participant-initiated break channel; (5) pre-register the coding protocol, the saturation stopping rule (or N justification), the thematic consolidation step, and the clustering sensitivity analyses; (6) specify the recruitment matrix (diagnosis, medication, gender, comorbidity, recruitment source) and the stratified reporting plan. [HUMAN_REQUIRED]
